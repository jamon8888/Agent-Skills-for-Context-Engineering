# Champion Signals: Behavioral Indicators

## Positive Champion Signals (Champion is Healthy)

These behaviors indicate an engaged, capable champion. Reward and reinforce them.

| Signal | What It Means | How to Respond |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| Shares internal org chart or politics | Trust is high | Reciprocate: share something sensitive about your roadmap or pricing flexibility |
| Introduces you to their boss unprompted | Power + commitment | Prepare an executive-level brief for that meeting; don't wing it |
| Tells you about a competing initiative | Coaching you | Ask: "What would need to happen for this to take priority over that?" |
| Forwards your emails internally | Acting as internal sales rep | Give them better content: executive summary, business case template |
| Pushes back on your approach | Invested in success | Welcome this; it means they're thinking like an owner |
| Calls/messages outside of scheduled meetings | Motivated | Available for quick coaching sessions; ask "What's the latest?" |
| Uses "we" language about the project | Identity investment | Reinforce their ownership; ask for their input on success metrics |

## The Champion's Personal Win

Every champion needs a personal win from the deal. Ask yourself: what does this person get if the project succeeds?

| Champion Profile | Likely Personal Win | How to Reinforce |
|-----------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Ambitious manager | Promotion trigger, visible win | Position project as leadership proof point |
| Overworked ops lead | Time savings, reduced pain | Quantify hours saved per week; make it their ROI story |
| New executive (hired < 12 months) | Quick win to establish credibility | Accelerate: propose a phased delivery with early results |
| Technical lead | Technical credibility, career development | Involve them in solution design; credit them in case study |
| Finance champion | Budget efficiency, risk mitigation | ROI model in their language; show cost of inaction |

**Key Question to Ask**: "If this project succeeds, what does that mean for you personally?"
