# Proto-Persona Examples

5 example proto-personas across different markets to use as templates.

## Example 1: Solopreneur Designer (B2B Services)

```markdown
# Solopreneur Sarah (Proto-Persona)

> **Status:** Hypothesis (Not validated)  
> **Created:** 2026-02-13  
> **Market:** B2B Design Services

## Demographics

- **Age:** 32-38
- **Location:** Major US cities (SF, NYC, Austin, Denver)
- **Job Title:** Freelance Designer / Design Consultant
- **Company Size:** Solopreneur
- **Income:** $80K-150K/year

## Bio

Sarah runs a one-person design consultancy serving B2B SaaS companies. She works 50+ hours/week but only bills for 30. Mornings are for client work, afternoons for admin (invoicing, proposals, follow-ups).

## Goals

- Build a $200K/year business without hiring
- Work with 5-7 high-quality clients (not 20+ small projects)
- Be recognized as a go-to expert in B2B SaaS design

## Pain Points

1. **Time on admin** — 10-15 hours/week on non-billable tasks
2. **Cash flow unpredictability** — Clients pay 15-45 days late
3. **Tool overload** — Uses 7 different tools, constant context switching

## Jobs to Be Done

- When a client asks for a proposal, I want to generate it in 10 minutes, so I can respond quickly
- When an invoice is overdue, I want automated reminders, so I don't chase payments manually

## Behaviors

- **Tools:** Notion, HubSpot Free, Stripe, Figma, Calendly
- **Sources:** Reddit (r/solopreneurs), Twitter, YouTube
- **Buying:** Free trials, G2 reviews, community recommendations

## Influences

- Follows: Pieter Levels, Justin Welsh, Arvid Kahl
- Aspires to: $500K-1M/year one-person business

## Quotes

> "I'm great at design, but I spend more time on admin than actual work."
> "I don't need a complex CRM built for sales teams."

## Validation Plan

- [ ] Interview 10 solopreneur designers
- [ ] Validate: 10-15 hours/week on admin
- [ ] Validate: Willing to pay $20-50/mo for all-in-one tool
```

- [ ] Validate: Willing to pay for automation tools

## How to Use These Examples

1. **Pick the closest match** — Find the example most similar to your target audience
2. **Customize** — Replace demographics, pains, and goals with your specific market
3. **Validate** — Use the validation plan to test your assumptions
4. **Iterate** — Update the persona as you learn more

---

## Template Structure

Every proto-persona should include:

- **Demographics** (age, location, job, income)
- **Bio** (day in the life)
- **Goals** (professional and personal)
- **Pain Points** (top 3 frustrations)
- **Jobs to Be Done** (what they're trying to accomplish)
- **Behaviors** (tools, sources, buying process)
- **Influences** (who they follow, who they aspire to be)
- **Quotes** (hypothetical, but realistic)
- **Validation Plan** (how to test assumptions)
