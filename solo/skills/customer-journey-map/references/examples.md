## Example 2: Service Business (Design Consultancy)

```markdown
# Customer Journey Map: Design Client (Small Business Owner)

**Scenario:** Hiring a designer for website redesign  
**Persona:** Small business owner, $1M-5M revenue

---

## Stage 1: Discovery

**Goal:** Find a designer for website redesign

| Element           | Details                                                                                                                 |
| ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Actions**       | ‚Ä¢ Googles "website designer for small business"<br>‚Ä¢ Asks for recommendations in Facebook group<br>‚Ä¢ Reviews portfolios |
| **Touchpoints**   | ‚Ä¢ Google search<br>‚Ä¢ Facebook group<br>‚Ä¢ Designer websites                                                              |
| **Thoughts**      | ‚Ä¢ "I need someone who understands small businesses"<br>‚Ä¢ "How much will this cost?"<br>‚Ä¢ "Can I trust them?"            |
| **Emotions**      | üòü Anxious (worried about cost and quality)                                                                             |
| **Pain Points**   | ‚Ä¢ Hard to evaluate quality from portfolios<br>‚Ä¢ No clear pricing<br>‚Ä¢ Too many options                                  |
| **Opportunities** | ‚Ä¢ Clear pricing on website<br>‚Ä¢ Case studies with ROI metrics<br>‚Ä¢ "Small business specialist" positioning              |

---

## Stage 2: Initial Contact

**Goal:** Reach out and get a quote

| Element           | Details                                                                                 |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Actions**       | ‚Ä¢ Fills out contact form<br>‚Ä¢ Waits for response<br>‚Ä¢ Receives initial email            |
| **Touchpoints**   | ‚Ä¢ Contact form<br>‚Ä¢ Email                                                               |

## Stage 3: Consultation Call

| **Touchpoints**   | ‚Ä¢ Proposal<br>‚Ä¢ Contract                                                                      |

| **Emotions**      | üòü Nervous (big investment)                                                                   |

| **Opportunities** | ‚Ä¢ Satisfaction guarantee: "Unlimited revisions until you love it"<br>‚Ä¢ Simple 1-page contract |

## Stage 5: Onboarding

| Element           | Details                                                                                      |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| ----------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |

| **Touchpoints**   | ‚Ä¢ Figma<br>‚Ä¢ Staging site<br>‚Ä¢ Email                                          |

**Key Insights:**

- **Moments of delight:** Fast proposal, clear timeline, training included
- **Moments of friction:** Slow initial response, unclear pricing, no post-launch support
- **Top opportunities:** Fast proposal turnaround, satisfaction guarantee, ongoing relationship
```

**Scenario:** Discovering and purchasing skincare product  
**Persona:** Health-conscious millennial, 25-35, female

## How to Use These Examples

1. **Pick the closest match** ‚Äî B2B SaaS, Service, or E-commerce
2. **Customize stages** ‚Äî Adjust stages to match your specific customer journey
3. **Add your data** ‚Äî Replace hypothetical insights with real user feedback
4. **Prioritize opportunities** ‚Äî Focus on highest-impact, lowest-effort improvements

---

## Template Structure

Every journey map should include:

- **Stages** (5-7 stages from awareness to advocacy)
- **Actions** (what they do at each stage)
- **Touchpoints** (where they interact with you)
- **Thoughts** (what they're thinking)
- **Emotions** (how they feel)
- **Pain points** (what frustrates them)
- **Opportunities** (how to improve)
