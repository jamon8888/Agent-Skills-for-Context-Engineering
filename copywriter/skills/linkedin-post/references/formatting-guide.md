Over 80% of LinkedIn users are on mobile. A dense paragraph that looks fine on a desktop is a "Wall of Text" on a phone.

### The Rule of Two:

Never have more than two lines of text in a single paragraph.

- **Line 1**: The Hook/Intro.
- **Line 2**: The Context.
- **(Blank Line)**: The "Breather."

LinkedIn measures "Dwell Time"â€”the amount of time a user spends looking at your post. Whitespace forces the user to flick their thumb more often, which increases the time spent on the asset.

### Proper Spacing Patterns:

- **Phase 1 (The Setup)**: Short, punchy lines.
- **Phase 2 (The Meat)**: Bullet points or slightly longer (2-line) blocks.
- **Phase 3 (The CTA)**: Isolated, high-contrast links or questions.

## ðŸ§ª 4. The "No-Slop" Writing Protocol

AI-generated content often uses "Bridge Sentences" that add zero value. Formatting helps you identify and kill them.

### What to Cut:

- "It is important to remember that..."
- "In conclusion, we can see..."
- "Let's delve deeper into..."
- "Furthermore, it is worth noting..."

### Use "Knowledge Nuggets" Instead:

Instead of a long paragraph explaining a concept, use a **Framework Block**:

> **The 3-Step Protocol:**
>
> 1. [Action A]
> 2. [Action B]
> 3. [Result C]
