# Search Use Cases: 50+ Example Queries

Organized by research goal with example queries and expected outputs.

---

## 1. Market Research

### Market Size

**Query:** "What is the total addressable market for [product category] in [year]? Include market size, growth rate, and key segments."

**Example:**

```
"What is the total addressable market for solopreneur productivity tools in 2026?
Include market size, growth rate, and key segments."
```

**Expected output:** Market reports, industry analysis, TAM/SAM/SOM data

---

### Market Trends

**Query:** "What are the emerging trends in [industry/market] based on recent [signals]?"

**Example:**

```
"What are the emerging trends in AI-powered productivity tools based on recent
product launches and funding announcements?"
```

**Expected output:** Trend reports, analysis articles, expert predictions

---

### Customer Pain Points

**Query:** "What are the biggest pain points for [target audience] when [doing task]?"

**Example:**

```
"What are the biggest pain points for solopreneurs when managing client relationships
and invoicing?"
```

**Expected output:** Reddit discussions, forum posts, user complaints

## Tips for Effective Queries

1. **Be specific** — Include context, constraints, and desired outcome
2. **Use full sentences** — Neural search understands intent, not just keywords
3. **Include timeframes** — "in 2026", "over the past year", "recently"
4. **Specify format** — "Include X, Y, Z" to get structured results
5. **Add audience context** — "for solopreneurs", "for Series A startups"
6. **Combine multiple angles** — Problem + solution + market size in one query
